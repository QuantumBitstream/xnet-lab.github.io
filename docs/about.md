### About XIS Lab

XIS Lab is a deep tech research and innovation centre housed within the
**James Watt School of Engineering** and **School of Computing Science**
at the University of Glasgow.

#### Our Mission

Our mission is to engineer intelligent, secure, and adaptive technologies
that bridge the physical and digital domains. We operate at the confluence
of telecommunications, electromagnetic systems, human-machine interaction,
applied AI, and cyber-physical integration — delivering solutions with
real-world industrial impact.

We collaborate with industry to fast-track Technology Readiness Levels (TRLs)
and shape the future of autonomous connectivity, cyber-physical systems
integration, and human-centric innovation.

#### Our History

XIS Lab was established within the James Watt School of Engineering at the
University of Glasgow. Since its founding, the lab has grown to encompass
multiple flagship projects funded by DSTL, DSIT, DASA, EPSRC, ESA, and
industry partners across the UK and internationally.

Key milestones include the launch of the EME Hub as the UK's National
Electromagnetic Environment Hub, the start of multiple DSIT-funded 5G/Open
RAN projects, and growing collaborations with healthcare, defence, and
energy sectors.

#### Research Philosophy

We believe in bridging the gap between theoretical research and industrial
deployment. Our approach emphasises:

- **Applied impact** — every project is designed to reach a deployable TRL
- **Cross-disciplinary thinking** — combining telecoms, AI, robotics, and sensing
- **Open collaboration** — working with government, industry, and academia
- **Responsible innovation** — ensuring security, safety, and sustainability
